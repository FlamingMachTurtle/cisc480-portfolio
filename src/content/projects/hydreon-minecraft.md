myContribution: >-
  I owned two specific pieces of the Lifeboat work. First, I wrote the
  pre-release QA checklists — explicit pass/fail items for core gameplay
  loops, item functionality, and map traversal — so that any change in the
  final stretch of a build was re-tested against the same baseline rather
  than spot-checked. Second, I executed the build work for several of the
  DLC adventure maps Lifeboat shipped to the Bedrock Marketplace — most
  notably the islands for the One Block Challenge map, and a cube-world
  adventure map where each face of the cube acted as its own up-direction,
  so traversal required parkour through trees and houses oriented sideways
  to progress around the edges. I didn't originate the map concepts; I
  owned the execution on those builds and the QA gates that decided when
  they were ready to ship.

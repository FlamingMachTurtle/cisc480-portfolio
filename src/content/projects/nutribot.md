soar:
  situation: >-
    Users with real dietary constraints — allergies, macros, time-to-cook — need
    personalized meal plans, but most existing tools treat these as filters
    bolted on top of a generic recipe database, and rebuild the shopping list
    from scratch every recipe.
  obstacle: >-
    Plans needed to be allergy-aware end-to-end (including substitutions);
    shopping lists needed to roll up across the whole week without duplicating
    ingredients; and the scheduling UI had to be intuitive enough that users
    actually finish planning instead of abandoning halfway.
  action: >-
    I designed the weekly scheduling UI, the recipe filtering workflow
    (allergens, macros, time-to-cook), and the shopping list aggregation that
    walks the week's chosen recipes and consolidates ingredients into one
    clean list.
  result: >-
    Users can plan an entire week in minutes with confidence that no recipe
    surfaces a flagged allergen, and the rolled-up shopping list eliminates the
    most common abandonment point of weekly meal planning.
